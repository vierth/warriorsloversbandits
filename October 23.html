Grades

I put up a bunch of comments and grades for you earlier this morning, and I'm really sorry for falling behind on this. Overall everyone is doing really well in this class and I've been quite happy about your essays. I hope it is been fairly evident from my grading, but I did want to talk to you about how I see particular scores so you know how to interpret the grades you are getting, because I know that they can be a bit anxiety inducing. Just so you know, I got plenty of Bs when I was in college (in fact, I got a C in freshman year Chinese).

I see an 85 as essentially exactly meeting expectations. There might be some gramatical and stylistic issues, but the contents are generally correct with no major misunderstandings.

Below 85 and down to an 80 it is still generally within expectations but might show more grammatical issues or be difficult to follow and contain misundertanding and mistakes.

Below 80 represents passable, but with significant issues.

Below 70 is unacceptable (doesn't come close to meeting the word requirement, completely off topic, plagerized, etc.)

85 to 89 are good essays contain fewer stylistic/gramatical issues that draw clear connections between all components of the essay.

90 to 93 are excellent essays generally free of gramatical issues and are clear without being too wordy. One tendency I've noted is to use more words than are necessary and to use passive voice. These aren't technically gramatical problems, but they do detract from the experience and a 90 and above will be less prone to these issues.

94 and above are exceptional essays that present clear links between all parts of the reflection (that is it is clear why all parts of the reflection are present) and provide clear evidence for all statements made and are clearly thought out and presented. Free of issues of style and gramatical issues, they generally make an innovative or unexpected argument or presentation (or if not innovative, very well thought out and presented)
