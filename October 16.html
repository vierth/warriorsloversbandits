Drama zaju 雜劇

We've been talkign about novels so far in this class, but today we are going to be discussing our first play. This was a genre that first emerged in the Song dynasty, but did not really take off as a significant genre until the Yuan dynasty.

These dramas are generally four act plays, and almost always have a happy ending (usually in the form of a reunion at the end.

Zaju is a mixed mode form in that it brought together prose dialogure with poetry and signing mixed in (when we see the names of songs these were the equivelant of musical notation).

Role Types

There are a number of standard role types in Zaju that define the shapes of characters. There is the sheng (the main male character, often a young scholars seeking to pass the imperial exams), the dan (the main female character, often a beautiful young woman and the main love interest of the sheng), the chou (the clown).

Wang Shifu

Very little is known about Wang Shifu. The only thing known about him in the latter part of the 14th century was that he was from Dadu (or Beijing). West and Idema suggests that he was probably a professional playwright, given the number of very good plays he wrote. Some have contested that he was most active from around 1297 until 1307, but this late date seems unlikley ecause it isn't clear why people around them seemed to know so little about him. Traditionally, people have argued that he was active sometime between 1230 and 1300.
