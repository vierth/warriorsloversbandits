Timeline for Chinese History
Here we can see the early history of China. The oldest times, the Xia were largely legendary, and the Shang marks the emregence of writing, often in the form of oracle bones and ritual wares. We'll be talking a bit about these in the other class. While this period is generally regarded as a dynasty, it was a lose conglomeration of independent states. It wasn't until the Qin dynasty that the whole of China was unified under one government. This particular dynasty subscribed to the legalist philosophy, which was the idea that one needed maintain control of the realm through harsh punishments and rich rewards. It only lasted through a single emperor, and essentially fell during the second empororer's reign. The Han Dynasty was established and lasted for four hundred years (with a brief interrignum from 9 to 25 AD.

Chen Shou and the Records of the Three Kingdoms

233-297. Lived in the kingdom of Shu but was exiled eventually for not showing deference to Huang Hao, a eunuch who worked for the second emporor of Shu. He was able to find work after the founding of the Jin dynasty, which was the dynasty that was founded after the uniificaiton of the three kingdoms. Note that official histories were produced after the start of the next dynasty. Chen wrote a lot of stuff, but the most famous was the records of the Three Kingdoms, which recorded the events of the Three Kingdoms period. Most of what we know about Chen comes from the official history of the Jin Dynasty, written over 300 years later in the Tang dynasty.

The Records of the Three Kingdoms

Was an official history (note that these were produced at the behest of the government and were mainly aimed at aiding the ruler in proper governance. It was a 65 volume history that was divided into three parts, one for each of the Kingdoms. Structurally, the history is a collection of biographies of important people and the things that happened to them during their life. The history was later extended by Pei Songzhi. This was a fairly common way of structuring history at the time, although manyt did include section that was chronologically organized, this particular history did not.

The Chinese Novel

There are a number of important characteristics that exist in Chinese novels of this time, and the Romance of the Three Kingdoms contains many of them. This is a chaptered novel, which was often read one chapter at a time. Each chapter begins with a couplet as a title that describes its contents, and each chapter ends with a formulaic phrase. We will discuss some of the functinos of these things as we continue to go through the noel. Note that sometimes the narrator of these works will interject

The Romance of the Three Kingdoms

Information from this history was rewritten into the Romance of the Three Kingdoms. It begins with events that precipitate the fall of the Han dynasty in the late 160s and gos through until the Jin dynasty reunifies the country in 280. The stories within the novel were not new (for obvious reasons), but they had been used for entertianment during the Tang and even earlier. In many cases these stories were told orally. In the 14th century a pinghua was published, which collected many of these tories. The romance was written in the very early Ming dynasty (probably toward the end of the 15th century, but was not published until 1522. There are multiple editions of the text, but the most famous was an edition edited and commented up by Mao Lun and Mao Zonggang in the 1660s. They introduced significant edits, shortening the work, changing many of the poems, and making the book more anti Cao Cao.
