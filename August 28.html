The Books

We will go fairly deep into the history of these books, but I'll introduce them very quickly today

Romance of the Three Kingdoms

三國演義

Ostensibly by Luo Guanzhong, this was first published in the late 14th century and is generally considered the first of the great Ming novels. This is a fictionalization of the history of the Three Kingdoms period, in the immediate aftermath of the fall of the Han dynasty

Journey to the West

西遊記

Written by Wu Chengen in the 16th century, this is similar to the Three Kingdoms in that it is a fictionalization of actual historical events. It tells the tale of the Tang dynasty monk Xuanzang (called Tang Sanzang in the story) as he travelled to India to retrieve Buddhist Sutras

The Outlaws of the Marsh

水滸傳

By Shi Nai'an (some people claim Luo Guanzhong had a hand in the authorship. This is the story of a collection of Song dynasty bandits who for one reason or another were sent to the Liangshan marshes for all sorts of wacky crimes. Song Jiang becomes their leader and they get into all sorts of predicaments, but eventually (in some versions of the story) rescue the Song government. Unlike the previous two, this takes historical peopels names but is not really based on any specific events.

Story of the Western Wing

西廂記

This is a big shift for us in terms of the style of the works, but I wanted to talk about this because it is one of the most important plays in Yuan China, and exerted extreme influence on later culture. It is a play by Wang Shifu Tells the story of love between Zhang Shen and Cui Yingying. It is based on a earlier Tang dynasty short story, which we will be looking at it class
